### Notes on stocks impacted by Covid-19

The COVID-19 pandemic, particularly during the initial lockdowns and market crash between February and April 2020, severely impacted industries reliant on human mobility, travel, and physical presence. The hardest-hit sectors included airlines, cruise lines, hospitality, energy (oil & gas), and traditional retail. 

Based on industry reports regarding the 2020 market crash and subsequent pandemic-era struggles, here are 20 stocks heavily negatively impacted, categorized by sector:
- Airlines
  - Norwegian Cruise Line Holdings Ltd (NCLH) – Experienced severe losses due to industry shutdowns.
  - Carnival Corporation & PLC (CCL) – Suffered massive devaluation as cruise operations halted.
  - Royal Caribbean Group (RCL) – Faced heavy selling pressure as fleet operations ceased.
  - Delta Air Lines, Inc. (DAL) – Travel restrictions grounded flights globally.
  - United Airlines Holdings, Inc. (UAL) – Suffered significant valuation drops during lockdowns.
  - American Airlines Group Inc. (AAL) – Heavily hit by the cessation of travel.
  - International Consolidated Airlines Group (IAG) – One of the worst-performing UK airline stocks.
  - EasyJet PLC (EZJ) – Flights cancelled due to travel restrictions. 

- Energy (Oil & Gas)
  - Occidental Petroleum Corporation (OXY) – Hit by falling oil demand and prices.
  - Marathon Oil Corporation (MRO) – Experienced extreme price volatility and drops.
  - Devon Energy Corporation (DVN) – Sunk in the initial 2020 drawdown.
  - APA Corporation (APA) – Suffered as oil demand plummeted. 

- Real Estate & Retail
  - Walgreens Boots Alliance, Inc. (WBA) – Identified as one of the worst S&P 500 performers during this period.
  - Unibail-Rodamco-SE (URW) – Impacted by lockdowns affecting commercial property.
  - Hammerson PLC (HMSO) – Suffered from failing property valuations.
  - ASOS PLC (ASC) – Faced massive volatility and eventual declines in demand. 

- Tourism & Entertainment
  - Marriott International, Inc. (MAR) – Hotel occupancy rates plummeted.
  - Hilton Worldwide Holdings Inc. (HLT) – Impacted by widespread leisure and travel shutdowns.
  - The Walt Disney Company (DIS) – Park closures and halted production affected revenue.
  - Cineworld Group PLC (CINE) – Movie theater closures brought business to a halt.
